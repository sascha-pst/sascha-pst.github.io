I used to think e-commerce meant buying things online. We were the customers; then we became the product. Data is a medium of exchange. An invisible currency that fuels business models, aggregates our online behaviors and dictates a reality presented to us by algorithms.

At the intersection of emerging technology and consumer promotion, data serves as the capital that funds product optimization and targeted marketing. However, this 'liquidity' of data creates a paradox: while it drives unprecedented efficiency, it does so through a framework of pervasive surveillance, fueled by a hypercapitalist imperative that prioritizes data extraction over individual privacy.
