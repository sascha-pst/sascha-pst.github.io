The protagonist, Candace Chen, is a Chinese American millennial working a dead-end job in the New York office of a Bible publisher, coordinating production with factories in Shenzhen. A fungal pandemic called Shen Fever sweeps the world, originating in China. The book explores themes that proved eerily prescient when COVID hit two years later. An immigrant narrative folded into apocalypse combining nostalgia and routine as survival mechanisms. Severance won the Kirkus Prize and the NYPL Young Lions Fiction Award and got a noticeable second life for obvious reasons.
